6. Use the Modular Exponentiation algorithm to find 123^9 mod 101. This algorithm relies on the product rule of modular arithmetic: 
    a*b mod m = (a mod m * b mod m) mod m
The algorithm first converts the exponent into binary and 123^1001 to a product of the powers of 2. Since 9 is 1001 in binary, 123^9 is the product of 123 raised to each of the appropriate powers of 2: 
    123^8 * 123^1 = 123^9
a) Each term is first repeatedly reduced mod 101 by squaring the result of the previous power of 2. Complete the process started here: 
    123^1 mod 101 = 22

b) The final result answer is mod 101 of the product of the terms that have a 1 in the binary number of the exponent. To save work here, you can take the modulo of intermediate products as you compute the modulo of all appropriate terms. Complete this step.
