In an inductive proof your job is to prove that some proposition P holds true for all n, where n is a member of S, a well-ordered subset of the set of integers. The underlying argument is "if P(k) then P(k+1), for some arbitrary k in S." (The variable is k by convention.) You then use a direct proof: Assume P(k) and show P(k+1). P(k) is the Inductive Hypothesis (IH). Showing P(k+1) holds given P(k) is the Inductive Step (IS). Finally, to show P holds true for all n, give a concrete case using the first element in S; generally P(0) or P(1). This is the Basis.
