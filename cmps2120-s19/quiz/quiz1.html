QUESTION 1
Let p and q be defined as follows:

   p: Your alarm goes off.
   q: You wake up.

Which statement below is logically equivalent to p -> q?

A. If your alarm doesn't go off then you do not wake up.
B. Either your alarm does not go off or you wake up.
C. If you do not wake up then your alarm did not go off.
D. If you are awake then your alarm went off.
QUESTION 2
Let p and q be defined as follows:

   p: You work out.
   q: You are strong.

Which statement is logically equivalent to p -> q?

A. One sure way to be strong is to work out.
B. You did not work out so you must be weak. 
C. You cannot work out without being strong.
D. You can work out and not be strong.
QUESTION 3
Let expression S be defined as:

   (p ^ ~(p ^ q)) -> q

Which statement is true concerning S?

A. S is a tautology.
B. S is a contradiction.
C. S is logically equivalent to (~p v q) ^ (~p v p) v q.
D. The premise is logically equivalent to (p ^ ~q) v (p ^ ~p).
QUESTION 4
Let S be this expression:

   "You must say the password to get into the club."

Which statement is logically equivalent to S?

A. If you get into the club then you said the password.
B. If you say the password then you get into the club.
C. If you don't say the password then you don't get into the club.
D. If you don't get into the club then you didn't say the password.
QUESTION 5
Let S be defined as this logical expression:

   ((~p v r) ^ (p ^ q)) -> r

Which statement is true concerning S?

A. S is a tautology.
B. S is a contradiction.
C. S is logically equivalent to (p ^ ~r) v (~p v ~q) v r.
D. S is logically equivalent to (~p ^ r) v ~q.
