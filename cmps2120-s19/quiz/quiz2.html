QUESTION 1
Study the following argument.

   1. Only computer science majors take a discrete structures course.
   2. Julie is majoring in computer science.
   ------------------------------------------------------------------
   ∴  Julie is taking a discrete structures course.

What is true?

A. This argument is valid.
B. All cs majors take discrete structures.
C. This argument is invalid.
D. This argument clearly defies the affirmation.
QUESTION 2
Analyze the argument below.

   1. You can major in computer science if and only if you take 2120.
   2. You are taking 2120.
   ------------------------------------------------------------------
   ∴  You can major in computer science.

What is true?

A. Invalid argument, affirming the conclusion.
B. This argument has validity.
C. Premise 2 should read, "You are not taking 2120."
D. I'm going to change my major to Art.
QUESTION 3
Scrutinize the following argument.

   1. I play my best golf when my mind is blank and I'm loose as a goose.
   2. I'm not feeling loose at all.
   ----------------------------------------------------------------------
   ∴  I will not play my best golf.

What is true?

A. This argument is valid.
B. Invalid because one of the premises is false. 
C. Premise 1 is too complex for analysis.
D. Invalid because you are denying the hypothesis.
QUESTION 4
Here is a statement:

   I either work or I sit and relax.

Which below is logically equivalent?

A. I work or I sit, and I work or I relax.
B. I work and I sit, or I work and I relax.
C. If I work, then I don't sit and I relax.
D. If I don't work, then I sit and I relax.
QUESTION 5

Look at the following statement and decide what it implies.

   I either eat or I relax, and I either don't eat or I sleep.

A. I relax and I sleep.
B. I either relax or I sleep.
C. If I eat then I sleep.
D. Eating does not relax me.
